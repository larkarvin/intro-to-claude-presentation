The big idea

You don't write
the code. You
direct it.

Think of it as a fast, tireless assistant — endlessly helpful, but sometimes confidently wrong.

Your job: stay in command.
Before the tools

Fundamentals
are king.

Learn the fundamentals of what you're building — so you know what the code actually does and what "good" looks like.

Tools change. Fundamentals don't. you can't direct what you don't understand.
The workflow

The loop.

01SpecSay what "done" means — in plain words.
→
02PlanClaude proposes steps. You approve before code.
→
03DevelopSmall steps. Run it. Refine.
→
04ReviewRead the diff. Accept what you understand.
Runs on a foundation ▸ a CLAUDE.md of rules · small commits · run it every step · never merge what you can't explain.
Slop vs quality

Slop ▾

  • Vague prompt, plausible output
  • You don't actually understand it
  • Accept blindly, ship it
  • Bloat, silent bugs, no tests

Quality ▴

  • Clear intent + constraints
  • Small steps you can follow
  • You read & verify every change
  • Lean, working, explainable
The anti-slop formula
quality=
specify constrain chunk scrutinize
slop=vibes ÷ hope
AI gives you a draft, not a decision.
